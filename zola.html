Case Study: Real Weddings photo uploader for Vendors

Real Weddings is a Pinterest-esk expereince for couples to explore a broad range of photos from real weddings that our vendors have worked.

The problem

The Real Weddings product showed a lot of potential to bring in early funnel users and retain them in our planning ecosystem. The problem was that all of our Real Weddings content was very old. If we wanted to better leverage this piece of our product to attract users, then we were going to need a lot more fresh content.

The solution

The best way to get new, fresh content that would be relevant to our couples was from our vendors. By creating a new experience for photographers to upload their work to Zola, we could increase our content supply and help create a direct connection between the content and vendors on our platform.

Business & product needs

Business and product stakeholders needed content to drive user engagement and increase early funnel user retention.

Project goals
  • Create an easy uploading expereince for photographers
  • Notify photographers about this new tool and educate them on how to use it
  • Incentivize photographers to upload content
  • Create a way for Zola to moderate which content goes up on the site

Research

How I approached gaining an understanding of our users' needs and how other people have solved this same problem.

Competitive Audit

Submitting real wedding content is a regular practice for most wedding photographers, so it was essential that I understand the tools and flows they are used to using.

To do this, I looked at the live experiences living on competitors sites, such as The Knot, Carats & Cake, and Style Me Pretty.

User research
  • Vendors: I gathered user feedback with our quarterly vendor survey and with in-person interviews with 5 vendors on the platform.
  • Internal resources: While designing and iterating on the expereince, I reached out to our Vendor Experience team—a group of individuals who are in constant contact with our vendors and have a thorough understanding of their needs.
  • UserTesting.com: To validate the usability of the flow, I sent it through user testing several time during the design process.

Design process

How I approached creating an upload experience that our photographers would love, while balancing the business and product needs of Zola.

Understanding user needs

Couples want to be able to easily browse real weddings on Zola and find the vendors that made their favorite parts happen.

Photographers wanted to improve their discoverability, showcase their work, and get more inquiries from couples.

Beginning the design process

Drew wire frames and user flow diagrams, to establish a user journey that incorporated each requirement outlined by product, business, and tech.

Closely worked with engineers to understand how I could design for the best experience and easiest build.

Developed robust prototypes for user testing and design reviews with stakeholders.

Reviewed designs with Business, Product, Design, and Engineering stakeholders regularly to ensure alignement from inception to design completion.

Finalizing designs

After interating based on initial feedback and rounds of user testing, high-fidelity designs began to take shape.

A final review with the complete team of stakeholders resulted in a few minor adjustments and final approval of the designs.

Preparing the Figma file for handoff to developers was the final step.

Project outcomes & key learnings

Project outcomes

Upon launch of this uploader tool, we saw immediate interest and adoption from the photographers on our platform. Due to this work, we were able to exponentially increase the amount of content in our Real Weddings product and create the business opportunity to redesgin to Couple's Real Weddings expereince.

Key learnings

Moving fast and smart was essentail in the course of this project. With only a month-and-a-half to complete the entire flow and marketing assets, it was necessary to stay in close contact with my cross-functional teammates, iterate on feedback quickly, and validate the flow regularly through user testing.
